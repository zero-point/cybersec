 Background

As we move to an ever increasing mobile technology oriented environment, digital security is becoming increasingly important. More and more people are switching to digital means of managing their day-to-day lives, including online banking, electronic auctioning and even the streaming of entertainment media over the Internet.

Loss of client/customer data can have both financial, and reputational, devastating consequences. The number of high-profile data breaches has been on the rise over the past decade. And with the retirement of one of the most widely deployed operating systems on the eve of support withdrawal, it is surely set to continue into the next decade.

As a result, software engineers have an increasing responsibility to understand security issues in the applications that they develop. Not only are we required to provide a system with extraordinary functionality, but we are also required to safeguard against possible attacks that may take place when our systems are in the wild.

 What Will I Learn?

This system aims to familiarise you with the possible threats that may face any applications you develop when it is released into deployment.

At present, we aim to introduce you to:

  • SQL Injection
  • Cross-Site Scripting
  • Request Forgery
  • And much more
