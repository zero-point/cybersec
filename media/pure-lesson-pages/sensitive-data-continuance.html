As you can see in the address bar of your browser, despite our use of a password form, your password is now clearly visible to anyone able to intercept the request. Let's hope you don't use that for anything important! In order to avoid this eventuality we can use a different variety of HTTP request, the POST request, to remove your password from your address bar and implement HTTPS in order to protect the data during transmission.

Strong encryption, such as that provided by HTTPS, is a powerful tool in protecting sensitive data within and outwith your application. Consider what you learned in the SQL Injection lesson (if you haven't, get over there!) and imagine that you are responsible for providing the database but not the interfce to query it.
